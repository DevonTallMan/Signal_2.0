## 0. Project context and operating mode

> **This section was added in v1.1 per decision D-013.**

This charter describes a production POC. The project is currently operating in **academic exercise mode** for the project owner's self-study of AI-assisted project management. The artefacts produced are intended to serve as a reusable foundation should the project owner later activate the production POC.

**In academic exercise mode:**

- Phase 1 (legal foundation) work is captured as artefacts but deferred from execution
- No real user data is processed
- The GDPR, contractual, and regulatory risks documented in the risk register exist as study material rather than active exposures
- Phase 2 (auth and state) and beyond proceed as production-quality work, because the technical implementation is intended to be reusable if the production POC is later activated

**Re-activation to production mode is triggered by any of:**

- Inviting any external user (including students or test participants) to the platform
- Sharing a public URL with anyone outside the project owner
- Adding analytics that identify or fingerprint visitors
- Any commercial activity targeting students

When any trigger occurs, the deferred Phase 1 work re-enters the critical path and must complete before further user contact. ICO registration, DPIA finalisation, and privacy notice publication become statutory requirements at that point, not optional.

The remainder of this charter describes the production POC. Read it as the future-conditional state of the project. The current operating state is described in this section only.

## 1. Project summary

The Signal platform's user experience is being upgraded with a structured learning pathway, per-user progress tracking, and LLM-graded exam questions. This proof of concept (POC) implements the new pattern on a single topic page, *4.1.2 Computer Misuse Act*, to test whether the approach improves student engagement before any rollout to the wider platform.

## 2. Background and rationale

The current platform was rated by the client as clunky to navigate. The cyberpunk visual identity and the introductory framing of each topic were rated positively. The body content, generated by AI, was rated as poor quality. The remediation is a rebuilt content layer, structured progression through each topic's subsections, and assessment via mark-scheme-graded exam questions with feedback. The POC is scoped to one topic so the pattern can be validated cheaply before scaling.

## 3. Vision and primary aim

Ship a defensible, legally compliant POC by the end of August 2026 that genuinely tests whether linear-with-jump-permitted learning, combined with LLM-graded assessment, moves the engagement needle on the Computer Misuse Act topic. The deliverable is a POC that can take real student traffic. It is not a demo.

> **v1.1 note:** This aim applies to the production POC state. In academic exercise mode, the primary deliverable is the complete set of PM artefacts demonstrating how this aim would be achieved, suitable for self-study and for future activation.

## 4. Measurable objectives

1. Phase 1 legal foundation closed by 14 June 2026. ICO registered, privacy notice signed off, Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) filed, college DPO and safeguarding lead informed.
2. Authentication and per-user state layer live by 19 July 2026 with Firestore security rules audited and locked.
3. Computer Misuse content authored to teaching-grade quality, second-reader signed off, by 2 August 2026.
4. LLM grading calibrated within approximately ±0.5 of the project owner's manual grading across a 20-sample test set.
5. User Acceptance Testing (UAT) walkthroughs run with five or more students by 30 August 2026.
6. Pull-through metric (exam section reached) instrumented in analytics from launch day.

> **v1.1 note:** In academic exercise mode, objectives 1, 5, and 6 are deferred because they require live external parties or real users. Objectives 2, 3, and 4 proceed as planned because they produce reusable technical and content artefacts.

## 5. Scope

### 5.1 In scope

- One topic page: *4.1.2 Computer Misuse Act*
- Human-authored subsection content for that topic
- Six exam-style questions with mark-scheme-aligned rubrics
- LLM-judge grading via Anthropic Claude Haiku
- Per-user progress tracking with progress-percentage display on each subsection tile
- Account creation with age-routed consent (under-16, 16–17, 18+)
- Right-to-erasure and data-export flows
- Cookie banner and privacy notice

### 5.2 Out of scope

- Any topic page other than Computer Misuse Act
- Cross-topic progress aggregation
- Mobile application
- Open registration before UAT
- More than six exam questions on the POC topic
- Commercial integration with the MSM® offering
- Free-tier or paid-tier business model decisions
- Multi-language support

## 7. Key decisions to date

1. Progression is linear-by-default but the learner can jump to the exam section without completing all subsections first.
2. The POC is scoped to the Computer Misuse Act topic only.
3. Authentication uses Firebase magic-link (passwordless) sign-in.
4. LLM judge for grading is Claude Haiku for the POC, with explicit intent to revisit free or self-hosted small language models at scale.
5. Six exam questions per topic.
6. Feedback is graded against a multi-level mark-scheme rubric, not self-assessment or keyword matching.
7. Content is authored in-house by the project owner. AI is restricted to variation and drilling, not source generation.
8. Signal is operated as the project owner's independent project, not under the college's data controller umbrella.
9. Capacity baseline is 1.5 effort days per week.
10. Phase 1 starts on 18 May 2026.
11. **(D-013)** Project operates in academic exercise mode with production POC as the future-conditional state.

The decision log captures the options considered and the reasoning behind each entry above.

## 8. Success criteria

- POC is live and usable by end of August 2026
- Engagement time (per-user session duration on the topic page) measurable in analytics
- Completion rate (exam section reached) measurable in analytics
- Content accuracy signed off by a second reader with subject competence
- No GDPR violations identified by second-reader privacy review
- Five or more UAT walkthroughs completed with structured feedback captured

> **v1.1 note:** In academic exercise mode, success is measured by the completeness and quality of the PM artefacts and the technical implementation rather than by live engagement or compliance review. The criteria above apply to the production POC state.

## 9. Constraints

- **Capacity:** 1.5 effort days per week from the project owner, dropping during teaching peaks and rising during academic-year breaks
- **Calendar:** End-of-term reporting cycles, mock weeks, parents evenings, and adoption process commitments reduce realistic capacity at known points
- **Budget:** Minimal cash budget. ICO Tier 1 fee ~£40 per year. Optional second-reader GDPR review ~£200–£500. LLM API costs negligible at POC scale.
- **Tech stack:** Astro + Cloudflare Pages + Firebase Auth and Firestore + Anthropic API. No new platform decisions are in scope.

## 10. Assumptions

- The college DPO and safeguarding lead will respond within a reasonable window after Sprint 1 outreach
- No contractual clause prohibits operating an independent platform aimed at the same qualification the project owner teaches
- Claude Haiku will calibrate within acceptable accuracy on the authored rubrics
- Five students can be recruited for UAT walkthroughs in late August
- The chosen success metrics will reveal whether the new structure helps

If any assumption fails, the project plan needs to be reassessed at the next sprint close, not absorbed silently.

> **v1.1 note:** In academic exercise mode, the first two assumptions are untested because the relevant parties have not been engaged. They remain documented as production-state assumptions.

## 11. Top-level risks

Full risk register is maintained separately. Headline risks at issue date:

1. GDPR exposure as a sole data controller handling minors' data — **Deferred per D-013**
2. Content authoring sitting on the critical path
3. Firestore security rules historically a known weak point on this codebase
4. Capacity slippage from teaching workload or adoption process
5. LLM grading calibration failing to align with the authored rubrics

## 13. Operating principles

The project manager operates under the following standing aims:

1. Force decisions on time. Drift kills POCs more often than wrong decisions do.
2. Push back on the project owner's optimism, particularly on capacity and scope.
3. Surface risks before they bite. Re-checked at every sprint close.
4. Maintain a decision and risk audit trail so reasoning travels with the code.
5. Protect the developer's broader work-life position. The POC must not cost more than it is worth.

## 14. Explicit non-aims

The project manager will not:

1. Cheerlead or default to agreement
2. Produce status reports, RACI matrices, or other documentation that does not earn its keep
3. Hold the developer's hand on technical implementation decisions
4. Allow "I'll catch up next sprint" to become a recurring pattern without formal re-planning
